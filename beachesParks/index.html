Beaches and Nature

Beaches

NATURAL BRIDGES- This park and beach is an excellent vantage point for viewing shore birds, migrating whales, as well as seals and otters playing offshore

SUNNY COVE- Cove Beach is in a deep cove filled with sand in eastern Santa Cruz, CA.The cove has low sandstone bluffs on both sides.

DAVENPORT PIER BEACH- Davenport Pier, a frequently photographed landmark, is at the beach below the bluff, but it is not easy to get to

PANTHER BEACH- With its difficult access and hard to find location, Panther Beach doesn’t get the crowds associated with many other Santa Cruz beaches.

Parks

GARDEN OF EDEN- Picturesque wilderness area featuring a riverside loop trail & a natural swimming hole.

NEARY LAGOON- Abundant wildlife viewing. Peaceful place for a walk. No dogs allowed in wildlife refuge area.Neary Lagoon is an important part of the urban watershed for the City of Santa Cruz.

EAST CLIFF DRIVE- The newly renovated East Cliff Parkway, between Pleasure Point and the Hook, offers spectacular scenery overlooking favorite surf spots.

Trails

QUAIL HOLLOW RANCH- Scenic nature preserve on 300 acres with a visitors center organizing walks & educational programs.

OHLONE BLUFF TRAIL- This trail connects the parking areas at the east and west ends of Wilder Ranch State Park hugging the coastline along the edge of oceanside cliffs.

REDWOODS REBORN Fall Creek- It's a 8.5 mile loop trail located near Santa Cruz, California that features a river

Scenic Views

SANTA CRUZ WHARF- The wharf is known for fishing, boat tours, viewing sea lions, dining, nightlife and gift shops.

CAPITOLA- Capitola is a cute little village with an Esplanade with restaurants and some cute shops and a beach where Soquel Creek comes into the Bay.

THE LIGHT HOUSE POINT-Open daily, sunrise - sunset.The Point features a surfing museum in a lighthouse & is the location of Steamer Lane, the county’s most famous surfing spot.
